This is in reference to the popular article: [Just fucking use HTML](https://justfuckingusehtml.com/?ref=cfardev) hosted and posted by [Kyrylo Silin](https://x.com/kyrylosilin) and its recent counterpoint, [Just fucking use React](https://justfuckingusereact.com/?ref=cfardev), which was posted about a week ago by [Burak Can](https://github.com/burakcan).

Most engineers are people, and most people don't make it to the bottom of the article. So I just want to start by putting the most important part of Burak Can's reply at the top of mine.[^20]:
> “The problem isn't the tool; it's the idiot wielding it inappropriately.” ~Burak Can

I don't agree with the aggressive approach so my response is going to be presented in a more level-headed manner... at least until I start drinking this hot cup of coffee anyway.

Just to expose my bias, I enjoy the message that both websites are trying to portray. However, I think that these messages can really overwhelm someone who is just dipping their toes into web development. The first message (from my understanding) was meant to implore junior/senior engineers to stay curious by digging deeper into how the tools commonly used by corporations actually work. The second also attempts to covey an important message but it ultimately turns both points into an argument leaving readers confused.

It's important to note that these articles are (more than likely) meant for more advanced web developers. **If you take them literally, you will have a bad time.** That being said; *this* article is being written for those who are just getting started with web development and are wondering what the fuss is all about.

Let's start with a [table](https://www.markdownguide.org/extended-syntax/#tables) so that we can compare and contrast the message that the two posts are trying to convey:

| Message                               | JustFuckingUseHtml.com    | JustFuckingUseReact.com   |
| -                                     | -                         | -                         |
